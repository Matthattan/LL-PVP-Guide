A PVP Loomian is a Loomian that is trained to use against other players in the Battle Colosseum. This can be detemined by its Moveset, TP (Training Points) Spread, UP (Unique Points) Spread, its item and more. You may find "Standard" sets which are what many players use however some may choose to utilise a gimmicky set to challenge those standard sets and get an edge in battle.

There is no "one above all" Loomian when it comes to pvp. However you can use usage lists and viability rankings to determine which Loomians offer more in competitive PVP.

Majority of Loomians can use many sets whether it be for the same purpose but an original set or for different purposes such as Barblast having a ranged defensive variant to handle Cephalops. There might be an objectively more popular way to run a Loomian however: "acting like there is only one way to run something [is a] Bad mentality." When a new Loomian is released, it takes time for users to find the base stats of the Loomian, its movepool and what Loomians it would prefer to have a matchup with. These, and much more factors, are needed to make sets for a Loomian so don't expect ready-made sets immediately after an update releases.

TPs
TPs mean Training Points and are earned by either defeating Loomians (trainers and wild) or using Gummies. Training Points scale based on level: at level 50, 8 Training Points is equivalent to 1 total stat point and at level 100, 4 Training Points is equivalent to 1 total stat point. Energy is an exception as at level 50, 4 training points are needed to increase the total stat by 1 and at level 100, 4 Training Points increase the total stat by 2. The maximum amount of Training Points a Loomian can have is 500 overall but only 200 can be put into any stat. TP yield can be increased by using the 5x TP Loomiboost which requires boost Tokens and Protein Shake which can be earned from beating the Heavy Bag - at Gym Rats, Sepharite City - in less than 5 turns. Route 6/Rally Ranch is currently the most ideal location to earn TPs due to having specialised trainers for nearly all the stats. Energy TPs can be earned from Tamyra located in the water of Lotusun Beach or Igneols, Skilavas and Varis found in Igneus Hollow. Teddy (the last trainer on Route 8) offers 2 speed and 4 Melee Attack Training Points which makes him an ideal choice for experience points grinding as well as TP training.

UPs
UPs mean Unique Points and also increase the total stat points of a Loomian. However, Unique Points are a set value when a Loomian is captured/rallied between 0 and 40 but can be increased by 10 using shards and decreased by 10 using dark shards. Unique Points are also scaled based on level: Level 50: 2 Unique Points = 1 Total Stat Point Level 100:1 Unique Points = 1 Total Stat Point

Personalities
Personalities help boost the total stats of a Loomian. Wild loomians have access to pure (one) personalities or 1 negative 1 positive whereas Rallied Loomians have access to "Totemised Personalities". These are 3 combo personalities that take the form of "[Negative] [Negative] Very [Positive]" or "[Positive] [Positive] Very [Negative]" (See Rally Ranch). These are usually preferred for pvp use due to offering more stat boosts either for one stat or increasing multiple and why event reskins of loomians aren't advised to be used in pvp however there are exceptions (see Set Making and Teambuilding).

The Damage Calculator is a useful tool that allows people to create and test sets without needing to prepare Loomians in game. After all, it would be a shame if you made a set in game and it turns out it doesn't work. The format of the Damage Calculations may be confusing at first but its actually quite simple. Lets take a look at a Revenine vs an Oxidrake:

200 ++AtkR Power Cuffs Vengeance Revenine Fire Breath vs. 200 HP / 200 ++DefR Heavy Shield Oxidrake 111-131 (57.8 - 68.2%) -- 5.1% chance to 2HKO

200 - the amount of TPs invested in the stat, note that UPs are not displayed in calculations due to users preferring them to be 40 by default.

++AtkR/++DefR - also known as Very Smart and Very Clever respectively, this is the indicator to what personality is being used in the Calculations (if any).

Power Cuffs - if the attacker is using an item that affects damage then it will be counted in the calculation.

Vengeance - if an ability that impacts the attack is being used, it will be included in the calculation. Abilities such as Combustible or Noxious Weeds require the box next to their name to be selected for it to be counted in the calculation.

Revenine/Oxidrake - the Loomian attacking and the Loomian defending, respectively.

Fire Breath - the move being used, the damage calculator also checks if the Type of the Move corresponds to the Type of the Loomian so the appropriate ATB (Attack Type Boost) can be given.

Heavy Shield - the item being used to defend against an attack. Note that if the attacker has the ability sly, the item will be ignored.

111-131 - the range of how many health points can be deducted. Below the calculation shows the full range of possible health points that can be deducted.

(57.8 - 68.2%) - the range of how many health points can be deducted as a percentage

-- 5.1% chance to 2HKO - a summary of how many hits the move will take. Note that Moves such as Slap Down - which varies damage based on if the opposing Loomian has an item - is accounted for.

A Loomian is considered a check if it can threaten a hit on the opposing Loomian however switching in is very risky and can cause massive health loss or even fainting. For Example, a Brawny Vesperatu can One Hit Knockout (OHKO) a tender Falkyrie:

200 +AtkM Dark Essence Sharp Claws Vesperatu Slap Down vs. 64 HP / 0 -DefM Power Cuffs Falkyrie: 292-344 (169.8 - 200.0%) -- guaranteed OHKO



However, Falkyrie can use Gamma Pulse to get rid of Vesperatu if it decides to swap in:

200 ++AtkR Power Cuffs Awakening Falkyrie Gamma Pulse vs. 80 HP / 0 DefR Vesperatu: 508-600 (306.0 - 361.4%) -- guaranteed OHKO



Checks can be sorted into two categories: Soft Checks and Hard Checks.

Soft Checks
Soft Checks are Loomians who can only swap in a limited amount of times before fainting.
For example, Revenine can be used to check Hunder but Hunder can hit it with Thunder Chomp (for neutral effectiveness) before swapping out on the next turn therefore avoiding a Fire Breath:

200 ++AtkM Power Cuffs Power Jaw Hunder Thunder Chomp vs. 72 HP / 0 -DefM Revenine: 68-81 (45.6 - 54.4%) -- 53.1% chance to 2HKO



Too many swap ins from Revenine can result in Revenine fainting and possibly losing the game due to having no other Loomian to defeat Hunder.

Hard Checks
Loomians used as hard checks are generally able to swap into some hits and can even have recovery to help battle against whatever Loomian it is checking but one mis-play can result in losing your check.

For example, a Very Robust Luminami can be a viable answer to an Offensive Brawny Power Jaw Tyrecks since it has high Melee Defence, has recovery through rejuvenate and it's use of Maroon can 3 Hit Knock Out Tyrecks:

0 AtkR Luminami Maroon vs. 88 HP / 0 DefR Tyrecks: 72-86 (40.0 - 47.8%) -- guaranteed 3HKO



However, if Luminami uses rejuvenate too many times and has not enough energy or Tyrecks increases its Melee Attack with Sharpen, Luminami would be vulnerable to chomp which can 3HKO first due to Offensive Nimble Tyreck's higher speed.

+1 200 +AtkM Dark Essence Power Jaw Tyrecks Chomp vs. 200 HP / 200 ++DefM Heavy Armor Luminami: 80-96 (38.6 - 46.4%) -- guaranteed 3HKO

A Loomian is considered a counter if it can reliably swap into another loomian and defeat it. For example, Cosmiore-Cracked is able to swap into Barblasts's attacks:

0 AtkR High Explosive Barblast Toxiblast vs. 184 HP / 24 DefR Cosmiore-Cracked: 56-67 (27.7 - 33.2%) -- possible 4HKO

Cosmiore-Cracked would be able to set up Peace Of Mind which increases it's Ranged Defense making Toxiblast weaker and increases it's Ranged Attack allowing for Psycho Blast to hit harder.

200 +AtkR Cosmiore-Cracked Psycho Blast vs. 200 HP / 40 DefR Barblast: 132-156 (65.7 - 77.6%) -- guaranteed 2HKO


The example above is referring to a hard counter - a Loomian, regardless what set its running, that can swap in and defeat a Loomian. Some Loomians, such as Vesperatu, are advised to run a specific set to counter Loomians like Mutagon:

200 +AtkM Power Cuffs Heavy Fists Mutagon Telekinetic Sweep vs. 168 HP / 128 +DefM Vesperatu: 45-53 (25.4 - 29.9%) -- possible 4HKO

0 AtkM Sharp Claws Vesperatu Slap Down vs. 64 HP / 0 DefM Power Cuffs Mutagon: 102-122 (52.3 - 62.6%) -- 13.3% chance to 2HKO

Terminology

2v2 - refers to the pvp format where 4 people battle sending out 1 Loomian at any one time.

7x40 - 40 refers to how many UPS and the 7 refers to how many stats have those amount of UPs. Variations include 6x40 which means all UP stats are 40 and 7x0 which means all UP stats are 0.

Attack Type Boost (ATB) - 25% strength Increase on moves that share the same type with the Loomian. if the Loomian has Awakening, ATB changes to 50% and if the Loomian has Ace, ATB changes to 100%.

Chip(ped) - minimal amount of damage done to a Loomian allowing it to be within range of a fatal attack.

Coverage - when a Loomian receives a move of a different type to be able to hit a Typing/Loomian.

Doubles - refers to the pvp format where 2 people battle sending out 2 Loomians at any one time.

GPVP - (Alpha) Gleaming PVP Loomians. They're mainly used to show off as alphas aren't a necessity for PVP.

Meta(game) - what is currently being used within the colosseum, especially amongst competitive players.

Movepool - the moves a Loomian has access to, whether that be from level up, rally or Move Manuals.

OHKO/2HKO/3HKO... - One Hit Knock Out/Two Hit Knock Out/Three Hit Knock Out.

SA - Secret Ability which are rarer abilities that Loomians have. Exceptions include Harvesect Evo Line, Boonary and Swirelle.

Usage Tierlist Definitions

Broke/Ubers - Loomians considered uncompetitive or over-centralizing to the competitive scene thus banned in tournaments.

OU (OverUsed) - Loomians that have at least an 8.3% usage. Loomians in this tier can be used in OU tournaments and above.

UUBL (UnderUsed BanList) - Loomians that have a usage lower than 8.3% however banned from UnderUsed tournaments due to being too powerful. They can be used in OU tournaments and above.

UU (UnderUsed) - Loomians Loomians that have a usage lower than 8.3%. Loomians in this tier can be used in UU tournaments and above.

NUBL (NeverUsed BanList) - Loomians that do not have a usage however they are banned from Neverused tournaments due to being too powerful. They can be used in UU tournaments and above.

NU (NeverUsed) - Loomians that do not have a usage. Loomians in this tier can be used to OU tournements and above.
